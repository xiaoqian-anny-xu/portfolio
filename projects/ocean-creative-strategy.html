OceanOutdoor Creative Strategy

Capturing the essence of being an "Amsterdammer" for digital screens

Keywords: creative strategy, presentation deck, user research, content creation, ideation, visual storytelling, Canva design, customer insights, cultural representation, team collaboration

I attended the Plusplusone creative workshop, where I worked on real-life cases for Bol and Ocean Outdoor. Collaborating with my teammates, we developed creative strategies and ideations, which we presented to the clients. I took the lead in creating and finetuning the presentation decks and thoroughly enjoyed visualizing our ideas. In the end, I stepped into the role of director to produce a video based on our insights.

Project Challenge

Ocean Outdoor, a company specializing in digital billboards and advertising screens, challenged us to identify a subculture in Amsterdam and visually capture their feelings and definition of what it means to be an "Amsterdammer" on digital screens.

Our team selected the Heesterveld creative community subculture and developed a strategy to showcase their unique cultureâ€”highlighting the home-like environment that fosters active creativity. We then crafted a creative idea based on this strategy.

Our Approach - Strategy

My team (Charen, Stanley, Devesh, and I) explored the Heesterveld creative community, a vibrant and artistic neighbourhood in Bijlmer, Amsterdam. I conducted an interview along with Charen, repeatedly analyzed the transcripts, and reviewed social media content to uncover key insights about this community. We discovered that a sense of home and mutual support is crucial for artists to thrive.

Therefore, our strategy focused on recreating the sense of home and belonging that once defined this community, guiding our approach to strategy and ideation.

Our Approach - Ideation

Our team worked closely with Ocean Outdoor to bring our creative strategy to life across various digital billboards in Amsterdam. We implemented a phased approach to roll out different interactive elements and themes.

The campaign received positive feedback from both Amsterdam residents and visitors. Key outcomes included:

  • Increased engagement with Ocean Outdoor's digital screens, with an average interaction time of 45 seconds
  • Over 10,000 user-generated content submissions in the first month
  • Positive sentiment in social media discussions about the campaign, with many praising its inclusive approach
  • Increased brand awareness for Ocean Outdoor among Amsterdam residents

Lessons Learned

This project provided valuable insights into creating engaging, inclusive content for public digital displays. Some key takeaways include:

  • The importance of real-time, dynamic content in capturing and maintaining public interest
  • The power of user-generated content in fostering a sense of community ownership
  • The need for robust moderation systems when incorporating live, user-generated content
  • The value of multilingual content in engaging diverse urban populations

These lessons have informed our approach to subsequent projects, helping us create even more effective and engaging digital experiences.
