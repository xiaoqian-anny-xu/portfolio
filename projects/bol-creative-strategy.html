Bol: Het Grote Speelgoedboek

Creative strategy for Bol's Sinterklaas toys book

Keywords: creative strategy, inclusive design, presentation deck, user research, content creation, ideation, visual storytelling, Canva design, customer insights, cultural representation, team collaboration

I attended the Plusplusone creative workshop, where I worked on real-life cases for Bol and Ocean Outdoor. Collaborating with my teammates, we developed creative strategies and ideations, which we presented to the clients. I took the lead in creating and finetuning the presentation decks and thoroughly enjoyed visualizing our ideas. In the end, I stepped into the role of director to produce a video based on our insights.

Project Challenge & Overview

Strategy presentation deck

Bol, one of the most popular webshop in the Netherlands, gave us a challenge to make their Sinterklass toys book “Het Grote Speelgoedboek” to be more inclusive towards all kids.

Collaborating with my teammates Tafara Wooter, Shaquille Marcus, Nitya Nair, and Nenyasha Matapuri, we developed a strategy to make the book enjoyable for children from diverse backgrounds. We proposed the idea of including a calendar poster in the book, allowing kids to choose their own celebrations. The strategy and ideation received positive feedback from the client, and I thoroughly enjoyed designing the presentation decks.

Our Approach - Strategy

The client Bol asked “How can we ensure each child feels represented and recognizes themselves in our toy book?”

To answer this question, I conducted street interviews with over 10 parents and their children, exploring their impressions of the toy book, its usage, expectations, and their kids’ definitions of inclusivity and diversity. Additionally, I researched social media, news articles, and Reddit for further insights. I discovered that children use the toy book not just for Sinterklaas, but for various occasions.

Combining my findings with my teammates’ research, we concluded that children want to enjoy the toy book regardless of whether they celebrate Sinterklaas. A strategy was then formed: “Get Gen Alpha, Who doesn’t feel represented in Het Grote Speelgoedboek, To enjoy the content By decentering Sinterklaas and making it about celebrations in general.”

I took the lead in formatting and fine-tuning the presentation deck, which was well-received by the client.

Our Approach - Ideation

Ideation presentation deck

Following the strategy phase, we moved on to the ideation process. Our brainstorming session generated over 50 ideas, ultimately centering on the concept of a celebration calendar poster.

The celebration calendar, included within the toy book, allows children to personalize it with their own cultural celebrations. Kids can draw, write, or use stickers to mark their special days, whether they be holidays, small achievements, birthdays, or other events.

In addition to the calendar, we included a sticker page where kids can create their own stickers or use predefined ones to decorate the calendar. We also added a page for children to write down their goals and achievements. To further promote the calendar idea, we also conceptualized a short commercial video.
